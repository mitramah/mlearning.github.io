Definition of Terms

The definition of terms (keywords) that mentioned in this study are as follows:

Applications (Apps)

A downloadable type of web-based or device-based software, commonly used abbreviated form "app" refers to a particular type of application that is designed for a single purpose.

Distant Learning

Learning methodology that is carried out remotely by using electronic communication.

E-learning

E-Learning is learning to utilize electronic technologies to access educational curriculum outside of a traditional classroom, therefore; time and place are dedicated to learning.

Formal Learning

The systematic, intentional form of learning that is designed by the instructor of a class.

Informal Learning

Non-traditional learning that is not teacher-centered, and may occur at the will of the student inside or outside the classroom.

ICT

Abbreviated form of information and communications technology, but is generally accepted to mean all devices, networking components, applications and systems that combined allow users to interact with the digital world.

Mobile Devices

Any portable technology with multiple functions and capabilities, with the Internet access.

Mobile Learning (M-Learning)

The process of using mobile devices (such as iPads, laptops, tablet PCs, PDAs, and smartphones) for the purpose of learning at home or elsewhere (Ally & Khalid, 2016) . Mobile learning can be done anytime, anywhere.

Web 2.0

Web 2.0 is the current state of online technologies which characterized by greater user interactivity and collaboration, more pervasive network connectivity and enhanced communication channels. The earlier World Wide Web (Web 1.0) was the static, read-only, web content that can only be edited by the creator.
