Social Learning Platform--Edmodo

While the early generation of mobile learning researchers was obsessed with the careful promotion of activities for educators, the evolution of mobile learning is turning the focus on the personal learning needs of its mobile users. It is often argued that mobile devices are in particularly suited to support social contacts and collaborative learning - claims that have obvious relevance for language learning (Kukulska-Hulme & Sheild, 2008).

The most commonly used social networking sites are Twitter, Friendfeed, Myspace, and Google+, but Facebook has since become dominant. The ease of usability and the ease of adaptation are among the characteristics attributed to Facebook’s popularity among mobile learners. Facebook’s users have the opportunity to connect wherever they are. Edmodo is a social learning technology that was launched by Web 2.0 developments and mobile-assisted learning. The Edmodo network is developed as a social learning area that enables teachers to share content with students, colleagues, and parents. This educational technology is available both in web-based and mobile application versions. The differences between Facebook and Edmodo is that the latter is considerably focused on the learning. The online profile of Edmodo is very similar to Facebook. However, there is also an extended social learning area for assigning grades, administering questionnaires and quizzes, making announcements, assigning homework and developing libraries, etc. These features make Edmodo a learning environment that can be useful in mobile-assisted language learning (MALL). Based on Bicen(2015) research:

“ Edmodo provides a fun learning environment that enables students to improve language learning, improve success, and strengthen inter-student communication. ”

Edmodo could be used as an efficient tool in social-constructivist approaches which views learning as an active process of building knowledge and skills through practice within a supportive group or community (Sharples et all, 2006).
