Recommendations for Future Research

This study helped identify the current university students' perceptions and attitudes towards the use of mobile devices or more specifically, mobile-assisted language learning technology in the classroom. Based on the research the following suggestions are offered to support the efficient use of mobile technology in language learning:

  • Increase professional developments for faculty members to discuss the relations between research, practice, and policy. The teacher development should bring awareness to the teachers and explain the potential use of mobile devices in the classrooms.
  • The university websites and learning management systems should be updated to allow students to view the course content on their mobile devices.
  • Language resource pages should be updated with the information about the current versions of mobile applications for the language learning.
  • The university administration needs to create more possibilities for an open dialogue among students, and faculty about the learning opportunities provided by mobile devices.
  • The university should also be required to update their language labs or libraries to be more consistent with the educational demands of the modern language learners.
  • The school should develop a collaborative environment for students. Mobile learning could support Conversational learning by integrating knowledge descriptions across different locations which can assist the mobile language learners.
  • Critical thinking, as well as media literacy should be encouraged among students. The students need to be educated to grasp the media literacy standards in reference to understanding, analyzing and producing media. The research also shows that students who are media literate are better able to resist messages considered biased, harmful, or inappropriate (Jacobs, 2010).
  • Differentiating mobile learning from traditional E-learning by integration of a design methodology that makes use of a variety of delivery mechanisms as well as unique content interactions and social features and platforms.
