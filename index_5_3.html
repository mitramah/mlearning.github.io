Interactive Collaboration & Sharing Tool--VoiceThread

According to Lev Vygotsky's sociocultural theory (Vytgotsky, 1978) , social environment is critical in individuals’ cognitive development and learning. Integrating the emerging mobile applications into mobile friendly web conferencing can create a collaborative learning environment which encourages students to engage in active knowledge construction. Research on the text-based discussion forums has indicated its inefficiency in enabling information search and synthesis, social-cultural relationship development, or multimodal communication (Ke & Hsu, 2015). However, rising web conferencing technology, such as VoiceThread, can act as a multimodal, mobile-accessible alternative for asynchronous discussion forums.

VoiceThread(VT) is a web-based application that runs inside your web browser (no software to download, install, or update) and allows learners to transform collections of media like images, videos, presentations, and documents at the center of an asynchronous discussion, into a place for a conversation.VoiceThread(VT) also enables commenting using a mix of text, audio, and video recordings (Ke & Hsu, 2015).

The VT-based peer critique can engage participants in constantly articulating, checking, and constructing content-specific mental models which are an affirmation of Constructionism. The Constructionism learning theory is based on the learners being actively involved in constructing knowledge out of their experiences, especially when they are engaged in building objects (ibid, P:3). Technologies such as VT “potentially promote the integrated development of content and educational technology skills, which compose an essential competency for teaching and learning in the 21stcentury” (Ke & Hsu, 2015).
