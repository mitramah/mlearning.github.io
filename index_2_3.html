Differentiating E-learning from M-learning

The skills adopted by the new generation of learners have been expanded beyond traditional literacy skills and have forced the teachers to use 21st-century tools and pedagogies to address them. E-learning is one of these approaches. E-learning is integrated with modern technologies and comprises all forms of electronically supported learning and teaching. E-learning is an approach that uses computer or communication technologies to facilitate or enhance learning.

Electronic Learning is a subset of Distance Learning and Mobile Learning is described as occupying a sub-space within E-learning, which is, in turn, a sub-part of digital learning. The term E-learning is referred to both “synchronous” and “asynchronous” form of learning. It is naturally adapted to suit distance learning and flexible learning, but can also be used in collaboration with face to face teaching, in which case, the term blended learning is consistently used Behera (2013). Also, E-learning is considered to be “tethered” (connected to something) and presented in a formal and organized structure as compared to self-paced, un-tethered, and informal presentation of M-learning (Mehdipour and Zerehkafi, 2013). Although there are some differences between E-learning and M-learning, they are regarded to be closely related. (Georgiev et al., 2004) view the relationship as nested in one another. Most scholars believe that M-learning is simply the natural evolution of E-learning, which completes missing components such as the wireless feature. It is believed that M-learning is a new stage of Distance and E-learning. However, this opinion has some differences. E-learning occurred as a subset of distance learning; therefore they share similar terminology.
