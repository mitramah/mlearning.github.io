Online Learning Management Tool--Quizlet

The learning needs of the modern language learners are not fulfilled by the use of a traditional methodology. Language learners also think that they are constantly drowning in the ocean of vocabulary and that they can hardly make it to the shore. However, Quizlet, a free Web 2.0 online learning management tool, for web browsers and smartphones, helps the modern learners to incorporate their language learning into a social context.

The Quizlet flashcard system also offers a mobile app. Quizlet mobile app contains an impressive auto-define function when adding new items allows the user to see/choose definitions that were entered by other Quizlet users. (Godwin-Jones, 2011). The powerful flashcard program, Quizlet, provides the users with a variety of activities that can be personalized based on the learner's needs, and goals. The course activities can be accessed through their website or mobile apps. Through Quizlet, students can upload course content to create flashcard sets that later can be shared and edited by their classmates or other Quizlet users. Other features include games and quizzes to help students learn exam material. The Quizlet resources prepare students with the opportunity to rehearse and reflect on lecture material in to extend key concepts that may appear on their exams (Foti, 2014).

The portability of mobile devices converged with their processing speed have made them the preferred medium for accessing Quizlet as a tool to utilize the learning materials outside of the classroom. Furthermore, in some research sites, the students reported sharing their datasets with their classmates; therefore, “it can be concluded that the use of mobile devices in this context plays a role, perhaps indirectly, in encouraging sharing and collaboration among students” (Miller, 2012; Schuler, 2009).

Through resources such as Quizlet, students can cooperate with their classmates by creating study materials that can be shared by all users and that can be accessed inside or outside of the classroom. In addition, Schuler(2012) found that mobile devices facilitate learning ‘anywhere, anytime.’ This finding can be applied to Quizlet as a web-based medium which encourages the language learners to engage in the process of learning inside or outside of the classrooms. The mobile environment may influence students' relationships, which may increase their willingness to create online communities such as the Facebook groups. Quizlet app also peobides the opportunities for students to share information (Foti, 2014).
